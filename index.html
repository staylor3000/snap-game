How to Play Snap

Each player takes turns flipping cards onto a central pile. When two cards of the same value appear on top of the pile, be the first to hit SNAP! to win all the cards in the pile. The player who collects all 52 cards wins the game. In PlaySnap you compete against a CPU opponent that gets faster and sharper with every level — by Level 10 you'll need lightning reactions to keep up.

Controls

Click Flip Card or press the Space bar to play your card onto the pile. Hit SNAP! or press Enter the moment you spot a matching pair. The faster you react, the better your snap time — tracked in the stats panel so you can always chase your personal best.

Tips & Strategy

Watch the pile, not just the top card — snap moments can come quickly and you need to be ready before the card fully lands. Keep an eye on your false snap rate in the stats panel — false snaps give the CPU a free pile win so accuracy matters as much as speed. As the levels increase the CPU reaction time drops significantly, so prioritise getting your snap time below 0.5 seconds if you want to compete at the higher levels.

The History of Snap

Snap is one of the oldest and most universally recognised card games in the world, with roots stretching back to Victorian England in the 1860s. Originally played with a standard 52 card deck, the rules have always been beautifully simple — match the cards, shout snap, win the pile. The game became a staple of British childhood throughout the 20th century, typically played with illustrated children's card decks featuring animals or cartoon characters.

The core appeal of Snap has never changed — it is a pure test of attention and reaction speed, requiring no strategy or prior knowledge, which makes it genuinely accessible to players of any age. The rise of browser gaming brought Snap online in the early 2000s, and today it remains one of the most searched casual card games on the internet, played by millions looking for a quick, satisfying mental workout.

PlaySnap brings the classic game into the modern era with a CPU opponent, a 10 level progression system, and real time reaction tracking — keeping the simplicity of the original while adding just enough depth to keep you coming back.

How does the CPU difficulty work?
PlaySnap has 10 levels. With each level the CPU reacts faster to snap opportunities, giving you less time to react. Level 1 is forgiving enough for complete beginners while Level 10 will test even the quickest reactions.
What does my snap rate mean?
Your snap rate is the percentage of snap opportunities you successfully claimed versus the total number of snap moments in the game. A 100% rate means you won every single snap — a very tough achievement at higher levels.
What is a false snap?
A false snap is when you hit SNAP! but the top two cards don't match. In PlaySnap a false snap awards the pile to the CPU so accuracy is just as important as speed.
What is my best snap time?
Your best snap time is the fastest reaction time you recorded between a snap opportunity appearing and you hitting SNAP!. It is tracked both for the current game and across your whole session so you can always see if you are improving.

Yes. PlaySnap is fully playable on mobile and tablet. Tap Flip Card and SNAP! instead of using keyboard shortcuts.
